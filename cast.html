Paila Pavese

Alma: The Mother

Paila Pavese comes from a family of Theatre and Film Artists, who were either actors or voice talents. Daughter of Nino Pavese and grandaughter of Luigi Pavese, PailaPavese began to act when she was only twelve years old. After she graduated at the Silvio D’Amico National School of Art, she started off as young theatre actress in 1960 when she worked with Gabriele Ferzetti. After that, she acted with other famous performers, such as Gigi Proietti, Laura Adani, Vittorio Gassman, and Orazio Costa.From the early seventies, Paila worked with the Theatrical Company “Gruppodella Rocca” (Group of the Stronghold), of which she was a founder herself in San Gimignano in September 1970. Moreover, Paila directed the voice-over in the two TV series “La Sfida di Artù” (Arthur’s Challenge) and “Il Giudice e ilCommissario” (The Judge and the Chief of Police. Also, in 2005, Paila acted in “Madame Bonaparte” for a Radio Program called Radio2 and in a radio sit-com called “Nessuno è Perfetto” (Nobody is perfect) in 2006. In addition to that, in 1982, Paila acted in “La Biondina” (The Little Blond Girl) and in “Ulisse e la Balena Bianca” (Ulysses and the White Whale), inspired by the Herman Melville’s novel Moby Dick and other writers; importantly, “Ulisse e la Balena Bianca” was directed by Alessandro Gassman for the Columbian Celebrations of 1992. Furthermore, in 2001, Paila directed the voice over of “Il Trionfodell’Amore” (The Triumph of Love” inspired by one of Marivaux’s comedies, while in in 2002, she supervised the voice over in the TV serie “Dharma & Greg”. In addition to the previously mentioned performances, Paila acted in several other Theatrical Plays, the majority of which were directed by Antonio Calenda. Last but not least, Paila worked in many other shows, such as “Il DesiderioPreso per la Coda” (The Desire Seized from the Tail) in 1967, and “Le Mammelle di Tiresia” (Tiresia’s Breast) inspired by Guillaume Apollinaire’s surrealistic drama. Finally, it is significant to mention Paila’s acting in Fernando De Rojas’s “La Celestina” in 1968, Bertolt Brecht’s “NellaGiungladellaCittà” (In the City Jungle) in 1969, and a lot of other theatrical plays. In conclusion, Paila acted in “Delitto per Delitto” (Crime for Crime” directed by Alessandro Benvenuti, in “La Presidentessa” (The female President) directed by Gigi Proietti, and in “Prova a FarmiRidere” (Try to Make Me Laugh), directed by Maurizio Micheli.

Varo Venturi

Alvise: The Psychiatrist

Varo Venturi (Rome, 9 August 1956) is an Italian filmmaker, a scriptwriter, actor, and musician. In 1997, Varo began his career as a writer and filmmaker with the production of “Cosmos Hotel”, a short-film awarded at Sacher Film Festival and presented at Locarno Film Festival and Torino Film Festival. After that, in 1998, Varo produced his second shortfilm, “Don Giovanni” ￼ both actor and composer. Moreover, he acted in director Peter del Monte’s “Controvento” with Valeria Golino. In the meanwhile, Varo was a leading actor in Ennio De Dominicis’s movie, “L’Italiano” (The Italian), for which Varo wrote the script with the collaboration of Luisa M. Fusconi. In 2003, Varo began to shoot a feature film, “Nazareno”, produced with Luisa M. Fusconi and distributed by DeusFilm. In 2007, “Nazareno” is selected at the European Festival of Independent Cinema and awarded with the Special Jury Award in Paris (ÉCU 2008). In 2008, “Nazareno” happened to be nominated as “Best Prima Opera” at the Golden Ciak Competition. From 2009 to 2011, Varo produced and filmed “Sei Giorni sulla Terra” (Six Days on the Earth), in which, thanks to the help of Corrado Malanga, Professor of Chemistry at Pisa University, he considered the controversial theme of Aliens. In 2011, this movie is invited at the 33° International Film Festival of Moscow (MIFF 2013). Last but not least, in 2013, Varo began to work on a documentary, “Real-scienza” (Real-Science), which he has been filming in Italy recently.
